Competitions

Colouring Competition / Colour Mania

The Colouring Competition provides a fun and engaging activity for young children to express their creativity. Participants are given a themed picture to colour in, and the winner is chosen based on the use of colour, neatness, and creativity.

How this competition benefits students: Studies show that colouring can improve fine motor skills, enhance focus and concentration, and reduce stress and anxiety in young children. The competition also helps to develop children's imagination and artistic skills.

Handwriting Competition / Writing Wonders

The Handwriting Competition challenges students to showcase their best handwriting skills. Participants are required to write a given passage within a stipulated time, and the winner is chosen based on legibility, style, and overall presentation.

How this competition benefits students: Good handwriting skills can improve academic performance, enhance communication skills, and boost self-confidence. It also fosters creativity and develops fine motor skills.

Collage Making Competition / Collage Craze

Collage Making Competition provides a platform for students to showcase their creativity by making a collage. Participants are required to use various materials such as pictures, cut-outs, and other art supplies to create a visually appealing piece of art.

How this competition benefits students: Making collages improves visual and spatial skills, develops problem-solving skills, and fosters creativity. It also helps students to think outside the box and experiment with different materials.

Finger and Thumb Painting Competition

The Finger and Thumb Painting Competition provides a fun and engaging activity for young children to express their creativity using their fingers and thumbs. Participants are given a theme to work with, and the winner is chosen based on the use of colour, neatness, and creativity.

How this competition benefits students: Finger and thumb painting improves fine motor skills, enhances focus and concentration, and encourages creativity in young children.

Cartoon Making Competition / Cartoon Capers

The Cartoon Making Competition challenges participants to create a cartoon based on a given theme. The winner is chosen based on the creativity, humour, and overall appeal of the cartoon.

How this competition benefits students: Making cartoons improves visual communication skills, develops creativity, and enhances critical thinking skills. It also helps students to think outside the box and express their ideas through visual storytelling.

Why do schools participate?

Srijanas competitions offer several other advantages for students of all ages. Here are FOUR reasons why you should encourage your students to participate in our competitions:

Built to inspire each student

Each competition is designed to challenge students differently and encourage them to explore different forms of art.

The entry cost is super-minimal

We have competitions for students in all school class groups, from kindergarten to college. The entry fee for each competition is Rs. 50/- per student. We believe that our entry fees are affordable for all students, and we do not want financial constraints to prevent any student from participating.

Boosts confidence and self-esteem

Taking part in Srijanas competitions can help boost a student's confidence and self-esteem. When students participate in the competition and see their work recognised, it can boost their morale and provide a sense of achievement. This, in turn, helps students gain more confidence in their abilities and helps them become more self-assured.

Develops life skills

Participating in competitions can help students develop various life skills such as time management, decision-making, problem-solving, teamwork, and communication skills. These skills are essential not only for academic success but also for future career prospects. By participating in Srijanas competitions, students can learn and develop these skills, which can help them excel in other areas of their lives as well.

Who can participate in Srijanas competitions?

Our competitions are open to students of all ages and classes. Whether you are in kindergarten or college, there is a competition that is suitable for you. We believe that art has no age limit, and anyone can participate and showcase their creativity. We welcome students from all backgrounds, including those who have never had the opportunity to participate in an art competition before. We aim to promote creativity and provide a platform for students to explore their artistic talents.

That said, every age group comes with a different skill set and maturity in their artistic capabilities over the years. This is why students in your school can participate in any of the below-age eligibility categories, based on their classes of education at the school.

Tiny Tagores: Primary I: (Std. I & II)

Our youngest participants, the Tiny Tagores, are budding artists in their own right. They may be little, but they have big imaginations and creative spirits that are just waiting to be unleashed.

Junior Jamini Roys: Primary II: (Std. III & IV)

The Junior Jamini Roys are the next level of our young artists, who have already started to explore the world of art. They are gaining more control over their artistic abilities and experimenting with different styles and techniques.

Rising Raja Ravi Vermas: Secondary I: (Std. V to VII)

The Rising Raja Ravi Vermas are our middle school artists, who are now more confident in their artistic abilities. They are honing their skills and developing their own unique styles.

The Aspiring Picassos: For Secondary II: (Std. VIII to X)

Our high school artists, the Aspiring Picassos, are now at a stage where they are developing their own distinct styles and techniques. They are exploring different mediums and experimenting with various forms of art.

The Rising Leonardo Da Vincis: For Jr. College: (Std. XI & XII)

The Rising Leonardo Da Vincis are our college-level artists who are at the top of their game. They are refining their skills, and are already making their mark in the art world. They are the ones to watch out for, as they are the future of the art world.
